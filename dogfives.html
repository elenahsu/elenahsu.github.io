Brief

For this Interaction Design Methods course, we worked closely with a client for three months. The objective was to use and conduct a range of design techniques and methods to understand the conceptualization, user experience, and situated use that emphasizes design creativity and co-design.

Vision

Educational App
Garden Planning

An app that engages client involvement by allowing dog owners to stay connected to their dogs' progress. The features allow the client to have a greater understanding of the value DogFives provides through the transparency of the hike.

Client

Pick-up + Drop-off
Taking Photos
Professional Judgment

DogFives Hiking provides off-leash dog hiking services along the trails of Port Moody. It consists of two staff members: Martina, who is the founder of and hiker, and Teodora, who coordinates the administrative efforts.

Opportunity

Client Involvement
Customer Satifaction

We conducted extensive research on DogFives through numerous methods to better understand the problem that DogFives currently faces and to propose an effective design solution.

The data collected form the design research demonstrated a need for increased client involvement in their dogs' lives. With more engaged clients, the level of customer satisfaction towards DogFives would increase. This would hopefully reduce the number of negative client experiences for DogFives.

Insights

Ethnography

We were invited as guests on a hike where we observed the hiker's interactions with the dogs and the general execution of the hike.

  • Photos are the only method of demonstrating expertise
  • A sufficient representation of DogFives is not provided
  • An opportunity to encourage more client involvement within the dog's lives
Journey Frameworks

By comparing the journey framework of the dog hiker and an average dog walker, we gained insights into the actions, thoughts, emotional experience, pain points, and potential opportunities.

  • GPS enabled distance tracking that keeps an archived log of all the walks
  • Syste organization and indication of which dogs are in which photos
  • Keeping track of all the dogs simultaneously to make sure all the dogs are within range
Persona

By developing three personas, we looked into the motivations, goals, and frustrations of the dog hiker, a current client, and a potential client.

  • Client's goals involve improving their dogs' experiences
  • Clients desire a greater connection with their dog
  • Features that would encourage and allow the audiecne to use at their discretion
Cultural Probes

We designed and deployed cultural probes that were used to obtain greater knowledge of DogFives' satisfaction and dissatisfaction with its existing procedures, specifically with client interactions.

  • Client communication is an integral part in ensuring a fluid hiking experience
  • Trust must be established between the owner and the clients
  • Engaging clients is extremely important in developing a transparent relationship with clients
Participatory Workshop

We held three participatory workshops that helped demonstrate the importance of trust to be built between DogFives and its clients. This further elaborated on the need for greater transparency of communication between the parties.
